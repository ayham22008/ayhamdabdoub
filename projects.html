WAR OF KNOWLEDGE

حرب المعرفة

“War of Knowledge” is an educational strategy game that transforms learning into an epic battle of intelligence, strategy, and critical thinking. Set in the fictional world of “Wisdom Land”, players become rulers of four powerful kingdoms, competing through knowledge-based challenges instead of traditional combat. Build your kingdom, master strategic decisions, and prove that the greatest power is not strength, but knowledge.

Why This Project Exists

Learning Shouldn't Feel Like Homework

War of Knowledge began as a graduation project with one question: what if studying felt like commanding an army instead of memorizing a textbook? The game reframes revision as strategy — every correct answer captures ground, every mistake costs territory, and every subject becomes a kingdom worth defending.

Built around Arabic-Islamic art, architecture, and storytelling traditions, the project blends a respect for heritage with a modern game-design language. It was designed, prototyped, tested with real students, and iterated on across two semesters, from a single paper sketch to a playable Unity build.

The educational vision is simple: knowledge should feel earned, visible, and worth fighting for — not because the player is being tested, but because the player is being trusted with a kingdom.

The Legend

Story of Wisdom Land

Seven chapters carry the world from its founding to the moment the player steps in.

Chapter I

The Rise of Wisdom Land

Long before kingdoms existed, Wisdom Land was a civilization built on intelligence. Every person carried Intelligence Units representing their ability to learn, think, and solve problems. Knowledge became the greatest resource, shaping cities, libraries, and the future of humanity.

Chapter II

The First War of Knowledge

As kingdoms discovered the power of knowledge, ambition replaced cooperation. Debates became conflicts, and intellectual challenges became battles. Warriors fought using questions instead of weapons, but centuries of conflict eventually destroyed Wisdom Land and scattered its nations.

Chapter III

The Arrival of the Four Friends

In the modern world, four friends discover a mysterious signal that transports them into the broken realm of Wisdom Land. Each friend arrives in a different region, facing the remains of a world destroyed by the War of Knowledge.

Ayham Dabdoub enters the innovative East. Baha’a Jarar reaches the frozen North. Zaid Sawalha discovers the strategic South. Zaid Lallo arrives in the peaceful West.

Chapter IV

The Birth of the Four Kingdoms

Instead of conquering the broken lands, the four friends unite people through knowledge, leadership, and cooperation. From their efforts, four powerful kingdoms are created, each representing a different vision of wisdom.

The North represents strength and endurance.
The South represents strategy and truth.
The East represents innovation and technology.
The West represents preservation and memory.

Chapter V

The Age of Peace

For generations, Wisdom Land enters a golden era. The kingdoms exchange knowledge, build libraries, develop inventions, and protect civilization. However, Intelligence resources begin to decrease, and fear of losing power slowly returns.

Chapter VI

The Second War of Knowledge

The fragile peace collapses as kingdoms begin fighting for control over the remaining knowledge resources. Friendship turns into suspicion, and questions once used for learning become weapons again. The Second War of Knowledge begins.

Chapter VII

The Player's Journey

A new generation enters Wisdom Land to decide its future. Players must lead their kingdom, answer challenges, expand their territory, and prove that true power does not come from weapons, but from knowledge.

Under the Hood

Gameplay Mechanics

01

Question Battles

Multiple-choice, timed, and open-answer formats stand in for combat — accuracy and speed both count toward damage dealt.

02

Territory System

A hex-grid map of Wisdom Land where every won battle paints a tile in your kingdom's color.

03

Difficulty Scaling

An adaptive engine tracks streaks and response time, quietly raising or lowering question tiers.

04

Power-Ups

Consumable scrolls (50/50, Time Freeze, Double Territory) earned through streaks and daily trials.

05

Progression

Kingdoms level up independently, unlocking new abilities, cosmetics, and story chapters.

06

Victory Conditions

Victory is achieved by conquering the three enemy capitals or capturing the entire map. Use knowledge and strategy to defeat rival kingdoms.

Four Pillars, Four Thrones

The Four Kingdoms

Scroll through the realms of Wisdom Land — each built around a different virtue.

Kingdom I

Kingdom of the North مملكة الشمال

A frozen land of discipline and endurance where warriors train both mind and body. The North believes that strength begins with a powerful mind.

  • Leader: King Baha’a Jarar
  • Symbol: The Sword
  • Color: Royal Blue
  • Architecture: Frozen fortresses and ancient halls that represent the viking
  • Strength: Physical power and mental endurance
  • Ability: Battle Strength — increases combat advantage
Kingdom II

Kingdom of the South مملكة الجنوب

A kingdom of scholars and strategists inspired by seafaring cultures. Its people believe that knowledge and planning are the strongest weapons.

  • Leader: King Zaid Sawalha
  • Symbol: The Book
  • Color: Crimson Red
  • Architecture: shipyards, libraries,that reflect the pirates
  • Strength: Strategy and tactical intelligence
  • Ability: Strategic Insight — improves decision advantages
Kingdom III

Kingdom of the East مملكة الشرق

A land of innovation inspired by ancient Arab civilizations. The East combines creativity, technology, and intelligence to shape the future.

  • Leader: King Ayham Dabdoub
  • Symbol: The Gear
  • Color: Emerald Green
  • Architecture: Advanced cities and Desert towers that reflect the Arab civilization
  • Strength: Technology and innovation
  • Ability: Innovation Boost — enhances development speed
Kingdom IV

Kingdom of the West مملكة الغرب

A peaceful kingdom dedicated to preserving history and protecting the knowledge of past generations through endless libraries and archives.

  • Leader: King Zaid Lallo
  • Symbol: The Feather
  • Color: Desert Gold
  • Architecture: Golden libraries and ancient halls that reflect the western civilization
  • Strength: Defense and preservation
  • Ability: Knowledge Shield — protects resources
Who You'll Meet

Characters

King Ayham Dabdoub (King of the East)

A visionary inventor who combines technology and knowledge to create the most advanced kingdom in Wisdom Land.

Personality: Creative, innovative, intelligent.

Abilities: Innovation Boost — improves technology and kingdom development.

King Zaid Sawalha (King of the South)

A strategic ruler known for his intelligence and planning. He leads the South kingdom by turning knowledge and logical thinking into powerful strategies.

Personality: Analytical, patient, strategic.

Abilities: Strategic Insight — improves decision making during knowledge battles.

King Baha’a Jarar (King of the North)

A powerful and disciplined leader who rules the frozen lands of the North. Baha’a believes that strength, courage, and determination are the foundation of a great kingdom.

Personality: Fearless, disciplined, loyal.

Abilities: Battle Strength — increases his kingdom's combat power and defense.

King Zaid Lallo (King of the West)

A calm and wise ruler who protects the history of Wisdom Land. He preserves ancient knowledge and guides his people through wisdom.

Personality: Calm, thoughtful, patient.

Abilities: Knowledge Shield — protects resources and restores lost knowledge.

What Went Wrong (and How We Fixed It)

Challenges

Problem

Early playtesters answered fast and guessed, gaming the reward loop instead of learning.

Solution

Added a confidence-weighted scoring model that rewards accuracy over raw speed.

Problem

The four kingdoms felt visually similar on mobile screens at a glance.

Solution

Introduced kingdom-specific silhouettes, color-coded UI chrome, and unique ambient audio.

Problem

Teachers wanted custom question banks, but the original system required editing code.

Solution

Built a spreadsheet-import pipeline so any educator can add content with no programming.

Problem

Mid-range Android devices struggled with the original particle-heavy battle effects.

Solution

Rebuilt VFX with GPU instancing and an automatic quality tier based on device benchmark.
